The pricing doesn't reflect value. It reflects *dependency*.

When something is essential to your business—like knowing the true cost of an import before you order—you'll pay whatever the market bears. Competitors know this.

So they:

1. **Charge high entry prices** to filter for "serious" customers (which is code for "extracting maximum revenue from those who can afford it").

2. **Implement artificial feature fragmentation** so you can't get everything you need in the cheapest tier.

3. **Raise prices annually** knowing switching is costly and painful.

It's not a business model optimized for value delivery. It's a business model optimized for *rentier economics*—extracting as much margin as possible from customers with no good alternatives.
